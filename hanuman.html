"Mighty Hanuman, heart of devotion, strength unbound, in divine motion."


The Leap to Lanka

When Sita was abducted by Ravana, the demon king of Lanka, Hanuman vowed to find her and bring her back to Lord Rama. Facing the vast ocean that separated India from Lanka, Hanuman grew to an enormous size and prepared for a monumental leap. Blessed with immense strength and devotion, he soared across the ocean, overcoming numerous obstacles and challenges. Upon reaching Lanka, he shrunk back to a small size to infiltrate the city undetected. Hanuman’s journey was filled with encounters with demonic forces, but his unwavering devotion to Rama and Sita kept him steadfast. After discovering Sita in Ashoka Vatika, he comforted her and assured her of Rama's impending rescue. He then returned to Rama with Sita’s whereabouts, marking a pivotal moment in the epic saga.

The Burning of Lanka

After finding Sita in Ashoka Vatika, Hanuman allowed himself to be captured by Ravana’s soldiers to deliver a message to the demon king. Brought before Ravana, Hanuman advised him to release Sita and surrender to Rama, but Ravana refused and ordered Hanuman’s tail to be set on fire as punishment. Unfazed, Hanuman used his powers to lengthen his tail, wrapping it around buildings and structures. As the flames engulfed his tail, Hanuman leapt across the city, setting Lanka ablaze. The fire caused immense destruction, yet Hanuman remained unharmed. His fiery rampage was both a warning to Ravana and a demonstration of his unyielding devotion to Rama. After the destruction, Hanuman extinguished his tail in the ocean and triumphantly returned to Rama.

The Sanjeevani Mountain

During the fierce battle between Rama and Ravana, Lakshmana, Rama’s brother, was gravely injured by a powerful arrow. The only hope to save him was the Sanjeevani herb, located in the Himalayan mountains. Hanuman was tasked with retrieving this life-saving herb. Racing against time, he flew to the Himalayas, but upon arrival, he couldn’t identify the specific herb. To ensure he didn’t fail, Hanuman uprooted the entire mountain and carried it back to the battlefield. The medicinal herbs revived Lakshmana, allowing him to return to the fight. This heroic act showcased Hanuman’s strength, determination, and resourcefulness, further solidifying his status as a beloved and powerful devotee of Rama.
